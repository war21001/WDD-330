Week 1 Notes

Master Mobile UX from SitePoint

Rewire Your Brain

  • Focus on smallest, least powerful device = focus on the content and core functionality
  • Mobile and tablets are used more than laptops and destops
  • Whatever you do -DO MOBILE FIRST - start small and scale up
  • responsive does not mean mobile first - plan for future and ready for growth

How to imporve UX by Understanding Users

  • Be There - help along the way
  • Be Useful - if it doesn't work or is to hard ,the user will move on
  • Be Quick - single click, interactions smooth and seamless
  • Reachability - grip, hand position, view
  • Speed matters - variety of network speeds
  • Reduce image usage throughout sites/apps. svg/webfonts lower bandwidth
  • Optimize and minify css and js files - remove unused code and whitespace
  • GZIP files to eliminate unnecssary data

Better Mobile UX with Strategic Menu Design

  • Common used menus at bottom - easier to use
  • Placement matters
  • Understanding matters

Designing Intuitive and MObile Friendly Forms/Inputs

  • Ditch labels and have float labels inside the input field
  • Fields slightly taller
  • check boxes larger
  • paginate larger forms
  • auto complete fields
  • auto advance spaces for credit cards
  • give best keyboard for input
  • anchor tag phone number for easy phone call access
  • Easier you make it - higer conversion or sales>

Designing a Better Movile Advertising User Expierence

  • Ender users shape the market
  • Advertising on mobile is skyrocketing
  • Pop ups are depised and poorly placed
  • Make ads small and simple, video ads are becoming popular
  • Resize ad when click or put as background
  • Things to remember about ads:
    • Stop using pop ups
    • users don't visit your site to look at ads
    • keep them simple and sleek
    • make sure it's unobstrusive
    • content first
